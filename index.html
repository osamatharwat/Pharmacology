Introduction

Fluoroquinolones are broad-spectrum bactericidal antibiotics that inhibit DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV. They have excellent bioavailability and tissue penetration but their use is now restricted due to serious adverse effects.

Past vs Current Use

Used widely as first-line therapy for mild infections

FDA Safety Warnings

Tendon rupture, neuropathy, CNS effects, dysglycemia, and aortic aneurysm/dissection. Use should be restricted and based on risk–benefit assessment.
